# Stonetomb

At first, it felt as though the place had erected a silent barrier, refusing to let me in. My first three visits yielded nothing—I plunged deeper into its immense visual potential, yet left without a single worthy frame. I challenged its heavy geometries across multiple mediums, shooting on 135 film, 6x9 medium format, and 4x5" large format. It was only on the fourth journey that the ancient sandstone sanctuary near Melitopol finally opened up, granting me a handful of striking, honest images.

# Voices in the Mist
text: right
media: left

Once, I was fortunate enough to wander through its monolithic maze during a dense, heavy mist. In this landscape, human presence is fleeting; rare visitors appear on the periphery, only to dissolve instantly back into the gray stone, leaving behind nothing but disembodied voices that quietly vanish into the air. In those muted moments, the boundary between eras blurs, and you can almost hear the rhythmic breath of eternity.

# Traces of the Forgotten
text: left
media: right

As for the historical reality of this place, I know very little—I have heard fragments of its archaeological past, but chose not to remember a single word. I prefer the clean state of observation. Across thousands of years, a forgotten prehistoric steppe civilization left its marks here: I see ancient stone effigies (babas) guarding the horizon, and hand-cut millstones resting in the grass—proof that they once harvested grain and lived by the soil.
